Timeline: Julian Assange and WikiLeaks

  • NOV. 28, 2010 WikiLeaks discloses U.S. State Department cables - State Department documents reveal a hidden world of backstage international diplomacy, divulging candid comments from world leaders and detailing occasional U.S. pressure tactics overseas.
  • NOV. 30, 2010 Amazon.com stops hosting WikiLeaks - Amazon had been hosting WikiLeaks on its servers. After an inquiry from an aide to U.S. Sen. Joseph I. Lieberman (I-Conn.), it stopped.
  • NOV. 30, 2010 State Department removes files - The State Department removes its computer files from the government's classified network, Siprnet.
  • DEC. 1, 2010 Hackers stop WikiLeaks, temporarily - "You have ethical hackers who are really opposed to the notion that you ... decide what information should be disseminated," said Mark D. Rasch, a former federal cyber-crime prosecutor and now a security consultant.
  • DEC. 1, 2010 Interpol places Julian Assange on most-wanted list - Julian Assange is placed on Interpol's most-wanted list on rape charges after the WikiLeaks release of classified American diplomatic cables.
  • DEC. 2, 2010 Swedish Supreme Court summons Assange - The Swedish Supreme Court rules that Julian Assange, WikiLeaks founder, must appear before a magistrate in Stockholm to answer accusations of rape and sexual harassment brought by two Swedish women.
  • DEC. 3, 2010 WikiLeaks is forced to Swiss domain name - WikiLeaks takes on the Swiss domain name WikiLeaks.ch, after its American domain name provider, EveryDNS, withdraws its service.
  • DEC. 4, 2010 PayPal cuts off WikiLeaks account - The online payment service provider PayPal cuts off WikiLeaks' account used to collect donations, saying the web site is engaged in illegal activity.
  • DEC. 4, 2010 France moves to ban WikiLeaks from French servers - WikiLeaks temporarily finds a new home with French company OVH after an American company stops directing traffic to WikiLeaks.org. Then French officials move to oust it from its new site.
  • DEC. 5, 2010 Assange stops appearing in Twitter 'trends' - Twitter's "trending topics" are suspected of censoring tweets about Julian Assange and WikiLeaks from "trending".
  • DEC. 6, 2010 MasterCard attempts to block payments to WikiLeaks - "MasterCard is taking action to ensure that WikiLeaks can no longer accept MasterCard-branded products," a spokesman for MasterCard Worldwide says.
  • DEC. 6, 2010 Facebook stands by WikiLeaks - "The WikiLeaks Facebook Page does not violate our content standards nor have we encountered any material posted on the page that violates our policies," said Facebook in a statement, as it continues to allow content from a WikiLeaks "fan page" on Facebook.
  • DEC. 6, 2010 Significant' actions - U.S. Attorney General Eric Holder Jr. says he has authorized "significant" actions in the criminal investigation of Julian Assange and WikiLeaks.
  • DEC. 7, 2010 Swedish Bank Post Finance freezes WikiLeaks accounts - Swiss authorities close Assange's Swiss bank account, freezing tens of thousands of euros, according to his lawyers. WikiLeaks is still soliciting donations through bank transfers to affiliates in Iceland and Germany, as well as by mail to an address at University of Melbourne in Australia.
  • DEC. 7, 2010 Visa attempts to suspend payments to WikiLeaks - From the BBC: A spokeswoman for Visa Europe said its investigation would determine the nature of WikiLeaks' business and "whether it contravenes Visa operating rules."
  • DEC. 7, 2010 Judge denies Assange bail - A British judge sends Julian Assange to jail, denying bail to the WikiLeaks founder after Assange vows to fight efforts to be extradited to Sweden in a sex-crimes investigation.
